Sunny Side Up

Sunny Side Up is an exploration and platforming game that sees you play as an ordinary egg in someone's fridge. Try to escape from the looming fate of becoming their breakfast, and don't crack under the pressure.


Size of Team: 3
Engine: Unity
Language: C#
Platform: PC
Role: Lead Engineer

Notable contributions
Player State Machine
Lobby UI and UI engineer
Implemented networking using the mirror networking package and steam API

Issues and challenges
This was my first time working with networking, and initially, it took a lot of trial and error to create and join lobbies. The networking solution for this project uses Steam's peer-to-peer connection, as it offers the convenience and familiarity of most PC players.

The physics of the player have gone through many iterations. As a team, we wanted the players' movement to feel weighty but still give the sensation of rolling.

The player uses a state machine to handle all movements and respawning.

Things I would change
The physics for the player is not kinematic, which doesn’t allow for collision and rebounding between players.

Too Many Cooks


Size of Team: 3
Engine: Unity
Language: C#
Platform: VR
Role: Lead Engineer

Notable contributions
AI assistant Cook
Object storing system
Stove system

Issues and challenges
Initially, I was unfamiliar with developing in the VR environment. As I reviewed online resources, I discovered an XR simulator that significantly accelerated the speed at which I could test new ideas. I also played VR games to understand how developers let players interact with their environment.
For this project, the Idea was that you were in a busy kitchen, and to give that feeling, I made an AI Chef. I have worked with state machines before and found that AI generally follows the same structure. I implemented a G.O.A.P. or Goal-Oriented Action Planning for the structure. This AI works by setting beliefs, such as Boolean checks on functions, to determine where something is or whether it is being held. Then you set your goals that you want the AI to do, for example, keep the belief “MayhemSatisfied” true with a high priority. Then you create actions for the AI to use to achieve those goals. The actions have preconditions and beliefs that should be true at the end of the action. The action “ Turn on the stove” will require the AI to be in range of a stove and will satisfy the belief “MayhemSatisfied”. With these 3 parts, the AI can make its own plan based on the information it can get from its sensors and make its own list of actions to do to achieve and maintain the goals given to it.
